# Real Estate Industry Context

## Market Size and Structure

### NAR Membership
- Approximately 1.5 million active Realtors in the US
- NAR is the largest trade association in the US
- Agent turnover is high — ~50% leave the industry within 5 years
- Top 20% of agents do 80% of transactions

### Agent Adoption of Technology
- Adoption of new tools is slow but accelerating
- Clear Cooperation Policy changes and MLS consolidation are forcing digital transformation
- Agents adopt tools that directly help them win listings or close deals
- The champion model works: One enthusiastic agent convinces their brokerage, which convinces the MLS
- Key barrier: "What I have works fine" — agents need to see immediate, tangible value

## Homeowner Behavior

### Deferred Maintenance Crisis
- 60% of homeowners are deferring maintenance on their homes
- 43% of homeowners are living with needed repairs since purchase
- Average homeowner spends significantly less on maintenance than the recommended 1-2% of home value annually
- Deferred maintenance creates a hidden liability that buyers often discover too late

### Hidden Costs of Homeownership
- $21,400/year average beyond mortgage (Bankrate 2025) including insurance, taxes, maintenance, utilities, and repairs
- Most buyers dramatically underestimate total cost of ownership
- Performance-aware buyers make better purchasing decisions and save money long-term
- This data point is Pearl's strongest B2C messaging anchor

## Proptech Landscape

### Dominant Consumer Platforms
- **Zillow** — dominant consumer search, Zestimate is the "SCORE" of valuation
- **Realtor.com** — NAR-affiliated, strong agent relationships
- **Redfin** — discount brokerage model, technology-forward
- These platforms own consumer attention during the home search process

### Emerging Competitors
- **Home maintenance/performance:** Shipshape AI (maintenance tracking), RealReports (disclosure data), HouseCanary (valuation analytics)
- **Sustainability/energy:** Various state programs, utility company tools, IRA incentive calculators
- **None have Pearl's combination** of national scale, 5-pillar comprehensive scoring, and contractor certification network

## Industry Trends

### Data Transparency
Consumers expect more data about homes, not less. The trend is toward full disclosure:
- Home inspection data
- Energy performance ratings
- Maintenance history
- Environmental risk factors
- Pearl is positioned at the front of this wave — comprehensive home performance data is the future

### Sustainability Becoming Mainstream
- IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) incentives making energy efficiency upgrades more accessible
- State programs expanding for home performance improvements
- Energy efficiency is becoming a mainstream buyer consideration, not just a niche concern
- Pearl's energy pillar positions it at the intersection of sustainability and real estate

### MLS Consolidation
- The MLS landscape is consolidating (fewer, larger MLSs)
- This creates opportunities for data integration at scale
- Standardization through RESO makes it easier for Pearl to integrate with multiple MLSs
- Pearl's RESO partnership is strategic for this reason

### Clear Cooperation and Industry Shifts
- NAR settlement and Clear Cooperation changes are reshaping how agents market properties
- More emphasis on digital marketing and data-driven differentiation
- Agents need new tools to stand out — SCORE provides that differentiation

## Key Challenge for Pearl
Real estate is a relationship-driven industry. Technology adoption requires:
1. **Agent champion model** — win individual agents who evangelize to their networks
2. **Immediate value demonstration** — agents must see ROI in first use
3. **Integration with existing workflows** — SCORE must fit into how agents already work, not require them to change
4. **Trust and credibility** — NAR partnership, DOE alignment, and PR coverage build the trust needed for adoption
