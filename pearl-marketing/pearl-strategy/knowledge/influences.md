# Key Strategic Influences

These principles inform the spoke's strategic thinking. Reference them by name when they apply to a recommendation.

## Brian Moran (SamCart / 12 Week Year)

### Execution Over Strategy
Execution matters more than strategy. Compress timelines, create urgency. A good plan executed today beats a perfect plan executed next quarter.

### High-Leverage Activities
Focus on the 20% of activities that drive 80% of results. For Pearl marketing:
- Content that ranks (SEO compounds)
- Partnerships that open distribution channels
- Product experiences that convert (SCORE lookup)
- PR that builds credibility with decision-makers

### Systematize Operations
Quality should be consistent regardless of who executes. Build playbooks, templates, and automated workflows so marketing output does not depend on heroic individual effort. The Marketing OS is Pearl's implementation of this principle.

### Small Teams Win by Focus
Small teams win not by doing more, but by doing fewer things better. Ruthlessly prioritize. Say no to good opportunities to say yes to great ones.

### Periodization
Work in focused sprints with clear goals, not endless annual plans. The 90-day sprint plan is the primary planning unit:
- 90 days is long enough to achieve meaningful results
- Short enough to maintain urgency and accountability
- Forces regular re-evaluation of priorities
- Prevents "annual plan drift" where goals become irrelevant by Q3

## Wes Bush (Product-Led Growth)

### Product as Best Marketing
The best marketing is a great product experience. If the free SCORE lookup is compelling, it does the selling. Marketing's job is to get people to the product, not to replace the product experience.

### Reduce Time-to-Value
How fast can someone see Pearl SCORE's benefit? Every click, every form field, every loading screen is friction that kills conversion. Measure and minimize time from first touch to "aha moment."

### Free Users as Growth Engine
Free users are a growth engine, not a cost center. Each free SCORE lookup:
- Creates a data point that improves the model
- Is a potential word-of-mouth referral
- Is a future premium conversion opportunity
- Generates content (social shares, comparisons)

### End-User is the Buyer
In PLG, the end-user is the buyer. Make them successful and they bring the organization:
- Homeowners convince agents to use SCORE
- Agents convince brokerages to adopt Pearl
- Individual contractors convince their networks

### Bowling Alley Framework
Set up guardrails that guide users to the value moment. Do not rely on users to figure it out themselves — design the path.

## Jay Abraham

### Hidden Assets
Most businesses are sitting on hidden assets they do not monetize. Pearl's hidden assets:
- 92M+ home database (licensing opportunities)
- Contractor network (referral revenue)
- Consumer attention during home buying (high-intent audience)
- Thought leadership position (speaking, consulting, content licensing)

### Test Everything
Test everything, keep what works, discard what does not. Apply this to:
- Messaging (A/B test headlines, CTAs, value propositions)
- Channels (measure CAC and LTV by channel, not just traffic)
- Partnerships (pilot before committing to long-term deals)
- Content formats (which types drive the most conversions)

### Risk Reversal
Remove the barrier that stops people from saying yes:
- Free SCORE lookup removes "will this be useful?" doubt
- Free trial periods for agent tools remove commitment anxiety
- Money-back guarantees for premium features remove financial risk
- Case studies and testimonials remove "does this actually work?" skepticism

### Preeminence
Care more about the client's wellbeing than making the sale:
- Recommend what is best for the homeowner, even if it means less revenue short-term
- Build trust by being honest about limitations
- Position Pearl as the guide who helps, not the vendor who sells
- This creates lifetime customers and referral sources

### Joint Ventures and Strategic Alliances
Create exponential growth with minimal investment:
- Partner with organizations that already have Pearl's target audience
- Contribute what Pearl does best (data, scoring), let partners contribute what they do best (distribution, relationships)
- Structure deals so both parties win — if one side feels exploited, the partnership dies

## Digital Marketing Best Practices

### Content Marketing Compounds
Every piece of content is a permanent asset. Unlike paid ads (which stop working when you stop paying), content:
- Ranks in search indefinitely
- Gets shared and linked to over time
- Builds authority with each new piece
- Serves multiple funnel stages simultaneously

### SEO is Highest-ROI Long-Term Channel
For most B2B companies, organic search delivers the best long-term ROI. Pearl should:
- Invest in pillar content around home performance topics
- Build topical authority through comprehensive coverage
- Earn backlinks through thought leadership and data
- Optimize for both traditional search and LLM citations

### Email Owns the Audience
Social media platforms can change algorithms overnight. Email is the one channel where Pearl owns the relationship:
- Build email lists aggressively from every touchpoint
- Segment by audience (agents vs. homeowners vs. contractors)
- Nurture sequences based on behavior, not just time
- Email drives the highest conversion rate of any direct channel

### Social Proof Drives Conversion
Features tell, social proof sells:
- Testimonials from agents who won listings using SCORE
- Case studies showing SCORE impact on home values
- Usage statistics ("500,000 homes scored this month")
- Partnership badges (NAR, DOE endorsements)

### Attribution is Hard
Focus on directional data, not false precision:
- Multi-touch attribution is imperfect — accept it
- Look at trends and relative channel performance
- Trust leading indicators (engagement, signups) not just lagging ones (revenue)
- Invest in brand building even when it is hard to attribute directly
