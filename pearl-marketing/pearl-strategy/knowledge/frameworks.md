# Strategic Frameworks

Apply these frameworks by name when generating strategy briefs and recommendations. Always cite which framework you are applying and why it fits the situation.

## Growth Marketing

### Funnel Optimization
Awareness -> Interest -> Consideration -> Conversion -> Retention -> Advocacy

For each stage, identify:
- What content/channel moves prospects to the next stage
- Where the biggest drop-off is (the leverage point)
- What metric tracks progress at each stage

### Growth Loops
Content creates SEO -> SEO drives traffic -> Traffic generates data -> Data improves product -> Product creates content

Pearl's growth loop: Free SCORE lookup drives awareness -> Users share scores -> Sharing creates backlinks and social proof -> More data improves SCORE accuracy -> Better scores attract more users.

### Activation Metrics
The "aha moment" differs by audience:
- **Agents:** Seeing a SCORE in a listing presentation and realizing it differentiates them
- **Homeowners:** Looking up their own home and seeing actionable improvement recommendations
- **Contractors:** Receiving a qualified lead from a homeowner who looked up their SCORE

### Channel Strategy
Match channel to audience stage and intent:
- **Awareness:** SEO, content marketing, PR, social media, podcast appearances
- **Interest:** Webinars, thought leadership articles, case studies
- **Consideration:** Free SCORE lookup, product demos, agent presentations
- **Conversion:** Sales outreach, partnership agreements, onboarding
- **Retention:** Email nurture, app engagement, new feature announcements
- **Advocacy:** Referral programs, testimonials, community building

## Partnership Strategy (Jay Abraham)

### Value Stacking
Layer multiple value elements so the deal is worth more than the sum of its parts. In negotiations, always add value before discussing price. Each element should be quantifiable.

### Strategic Alliances
Partnerships where both parties contribute assets the other cannot easily build:
- Pearl brings: National data, home performance scoring, consumer awareness
- Partners bring: Distribution (agents), credibility (NAR/DOE), audience (realtor.com)

### Strategy of Preeminence
Position Pearl as the trusted advisor, not just a vendor:
- Lead with the partner's goals, not Pearl's
- Demonstrate understanding of their pain points before pitching
- Make recommendations even if they do not directly benefit Pearl
- This builds trust that leads to deeper, longer partnerships

### Three Ways to Grow a Business
1. **More customers** — expand reach, new channels, partnerships
2. **Higher transaction value** — upsell, premium tiers, bundling
3. **More frequent transactions** — engagement loops, subscription, recurring use

### Negotiation Principle
Always have more asks than you need. Trade low-cost/high-perceived-value items:
- Items that cost Pearl little but have high perceived value to the partner
- Items that cost the partner little but have high value to Pearl
- Hold back "give" items to use as concessions during negotiation

## Product-Led Growth (Wes Bush)

### Product as Acquisition Channel
Let the product drive acquisition: Free SCORE lookup serves as top-of-funnel. Every homeowner who looks up their SCORE is a potential customer. The product sells itself when the value is immediately apparent.

### Activation Focus
Get users to the value moment as fast as possible:
- Remove friction between signup and first SCORE view
- Guide users to their "aha moment" with onboarding flows
- Measure time-to-value, not just signups

### Freemium Model Considerations
What is free vs. what is premium:
- **Free:** Basic SCORE lookup, neighborhood comparison, general recommendations
- **Premium:** Detailed improvement plans, contractor matching, historical tracking, agent tools

### Viral Loops
How one user's action brings in others:
- Sharing SCORE on social media ("My home scored 720!")
- Comparing homes during the buying process
- Agents sharing SCORE reports with clients who then share with friends
- Homeowner sharing improvement results

### Bowling Alley Framework
Set up guardrails that guide users to the value moment:
- Onboarding bumpers: tooltips, guided tours, progress indicators
- Product bumpers: default views that highlight the most valuable data
- Conversational bumpers: email sequences triggered by user behavior

## Department Operations

### Lean Team with AI Augmentation
The OS model: Small team with outsized output through AI-powered spokes. Core competencies stay human; repetitive/scalable tasks go to spokes.

### Vendor vs. In-House Decision Framework
- **In-house:** Core competency, strategic advantage, needs daily iteration
- **Vendor:** Specialized expertise, cyclical need, faster time-to-market
- **AI spoke:** Repetitive, pattern-based, scalable, needs consistency

### OKR Structure
- **Objectives:** Qualitative direction (what we want to achieve)
- **Key Results:** Measurable outcomes (how we know we achieved it)
- Each quarter: 3-5 objectives, 2-3 key results per objective
- Key results should be stretch goals (70% achievement = success)

### Budget Allocation
- **70%** proven channels (SEO, content, email, PR)
- **20%** emerging channels (podcasts, partnerships, events)
- **10%** experimental (new platforms, guerrilla tactics, tests)
