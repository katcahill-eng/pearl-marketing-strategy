# Positioning Guardrails

Pearl's go-to-market depends on real estate agents feeling SAFER, not more exposed. This is non-negotiable.

## Frame As

- Independent standards and ratings organization
- Neutral information that belongs to the home, not either party
- Pre-inspection preparation (not a competing service)
- Early visibility — before impressions harden
- Expectation-setting and structured understanding
- Smoother transactions through organized information
- Guide to discovery (buyers)
- Mirror showing how the home already appears in public data (homeowners)
- Process leverage that keeps conversations on rails (buy-side agents)
- Risk management — surfaces performance topics before they become deal risk (listing agents)

## Never Frame As

- Buyer ammunition or negotiation weapon
- Seller weakness exposure
- Defect finding or condition diagnosis
- Pass/fail judgment
- Seller marketing tool or price-increase tool (Pearl 1.0 thinking)
- Surveillance or intrusion into homeowner data
- A guarantee of transaction outcomes
- A replacement for professional inspection
- A competitor to Zillow, MLS, or inspectors

## Prohibited Words and Phrases

**Near SCORE output — never use:** "defect," "flaw," "issue," "problem." Pearl describes performance characteristics and context.

**Anywhere — never use:** "low confidence" in connection with a SCORE. This phrase is retired from all Pearl communications.

**Near unclaimed homes — never use:** "inaccurate," "unreliable," "incomplete score." The score is accurate; the profile may be less complete.

**Near typical scores — never use:** "warning," "red flag," "concern," "mediocre." Typical means no significant performance concerns found.

**Near claiming — never use:** "improve accuracy," "fix your score." Use: "complete your home's story," "complete the record."

## Audience-Specific Guardrails

**Buyer content:**
- Do not lead with cost savings or utility bills (ranked last as purchase driver by buyers)
- Do not overwhelm — one score, five pillars, clear questions
- Do not position as inspection substitute
- Do not imply unclaimed SCORE is less reliable

**Homeowner content:**
- Do not frame as surveillance or intrusion
- Do not imply typical/low score is a problem Pearl created
- Do not promise claiming will increase sale price
- Do not pressure to claim before ready — invitation, not threat
- A typical score is good news, not bad news

**Buy-side agent content:**
- Do not position as negotiation tool
- Do not imply Pearl replaces agent judgment
- Do not introduce as additional complication — Pearl is a simplifier

**Listing agent content:**
- Do not position as listing price tool
- Do not imply Pearl creates disclosure obligations
- Do not use defect/flaw/issue/problem language
- Do not attack other tools or services
- A typical score is a transparency advantage, not a liability
