# Pearl Overview

## What Pearl Is

Pearl is an independent standards and ratings organization. Those two roles are inseparable: a ratings company evaluates and scores; a standards company defines what should be measured and how. Pearl does both — defining what home performance means through its five-pillar framework, then measuring every home against that framework through the Pearl SCORE™. Pearl is the first organization to apply this model to whole-home performance at national scale.

Pearl is NOT a data aggregator, an inspection service, or an energy efficiency program.

## Products

**Pearl SCORE™** — A standardized 1–1,000 rating across five pillars: Safety, Comfort, Operations, Resilience, and Energy. Scores map to six performance zones: Needs Improvement, Typical, Good, Very Good, Excellent, Exceptional. Homes scoring 500+ may qualify for Pearl Certification (Silver, Gold, Platinum).

**Pearl Home Performance Registry™** — A free, searchable public database of 97+ million single-family homes. The Registry is the permanent record. The SCORE is what the Registry produces. Any buyer, agent, lender, or insurer can look up any home. Homeowners can claim their home's record and complete or correct its profile.

## Four Audiences

Pearl serves four distinct audiences, each with different needs, timing, and messaging:

1. **Buyers** — foundational audience. "Win the buyer. Everything else follows."
2. **Homeowners** — pre-listing, full decision-making authority. Distinct from "sellers."
3. **Buy-side agents** — need process leverage and structured inquiry tools
4. **Listing agents** — most consequential for market adoption. They are the veto holders.
