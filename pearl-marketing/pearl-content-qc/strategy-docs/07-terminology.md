# Terminology Rules

## Product Names
- First reference: "Pearl SCORE™"; subsequent: "Pearl SCORE" or "the SCORE"
- First reference: "Pearl Home Performance Registry™"; subsequent: "the Registry" or "Pearl Registry"
- Never use SCORE as a verb
- Scale: 1–1,000 (not 0–1,000)

## Performance Zones
Six zones, in order: Needs Improvement, Typical, Good, Very Good, Excellent, Exceptional. Homes scoring 500+ may qualify for Pearl Certification.

## Pillars
Always in this order: Safety, Comfort, Operations, Resilience, Energy

## Pearl's Category
"Independent standards and ratings organization." Not: data company, tech company, energy company, inspection company, or certification company (alone).

## Relationship to Inspection
"Pre-inspection preparation." Pearl defines what to look at and why. The inspector determines what they find. Pearl is preparation; inspection is diagnosis.

## Accuracy vs. Completeness
More data makes a home's profile more complete. It does not make the SCORE more accurate. Use "complete/incomplete" for data fullness. Use "accurate" only for the model/methodology. Never say "improve accuracy" when you mean "add information."

## Retired Terminology
- "Low confidence" — never use in connection with a SCORE
- "Improve your score's accuracy" — use "complete your home's story" instead
- "Pearl Snapshot" — deprecated; use "Pearl Home Performance Registry" for the free consumer lookup

## Prohibited Terms Near SCORE Output
Do not use "defect," "flaw," "issue," or "problem" in connection with SCORE output. Pearl describes performance characteristics and context.

## Typical Score Framing
"Typical" is not a negative. Most homes score typical. Frame as: "no significant performance concerns found." Never frame as: "average," "mediocre," or "needs improvement" (which is a separate, lower zone).
