# Positioning Principles

## Foundational Truths

These strategic principles govern how Pearl thinks about its role in the market:

1. **The framework itself is the product.** Even without perfect data, the framework — what matters, how to evaluate it, what's known and unknown — delivers value. Value does not depend on data completeness.
2. **Pearl is buyer-first by design.** Credibility depends on buyer-alignment. If Pearl is perceived as helping sellers sell, it loses its value as a standard — for everyone, including sellers.
3. **The SCORE is accurate. Data completeness is a separate question.** Three distinct elements: the model (rigorous), the methodology (rigorous), and the data (variable). An unclaimed home produces an accurate score of what is currently known — not an inaccurate score.
4. **The blank spots have value.** Gaps in data are structured signals — showing where information is missing, why it matters, and where questions should be directed. Turns "I don't know" into "here is what I don't know and how to find out."
5. **Pearl is a translation layer.** Translates building science (BTUs, air changes, system specs) into lived outcomes: comfort, safety, cost, reliability, risk.
6. **Pearl replaces fragmentation with coherence.** Home performance data is scattered across thousands of disconnected databases. Pearl creates a single, coherent system.
7. **Pearl creates agency.** For buyers: from passive observers to informed participants. For homeowners: see and control how the home appears to the market.
8. **Pearl appears at the right moment.** Most valuable at the outset — before impressions harden, before inspection, before negotiation begins.
9. **The SCORE belongs to the home.** Not to the buyer or the seller. This depersonalizes difficult conversations and keeps Pearl neutral.

## Audience-Specific Positioning

- **For buyers:** Pearl is a guide to understanding a home — framework, vocabulary, structured questions.
- **For homeowners:** Pearl is a mirror — showing how the home already appears in the public domain, with the ability to correct and complete the record.
- **For buy-side agents:** Pearl keeps performance conversations on rails — organizing buyer anxiety into structured questions rather than scattered panic.
- **For listing agents:** Pearl puts performance conversations on rails before they become transaction risk — risk management, not risk creation.

## Anti-Pattern: Apologetic Positioning

Any content that apologizes for data gaps before demonstrating value is a **critical failure**. Specific violations:

- Leading with caveats about data limitations
- Using "low confidence" in connection with a SCORE (retired terminology)
- Framing data gaps as Pearl's shortcoming rather than structured signals
- Undermining confidence before demonstrating value
- Implying unclaimed homes produce lesser or unreliable scores
