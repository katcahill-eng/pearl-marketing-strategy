## Slide 2: Problem / Opportunity

**Real estate is flying blind on home performance.**

- Buyers shop on half the story — price, square footage, location, finishes — but not how the home actually performs
- 17% of first-time buyers can afford the median-priced home
- Average non-mortgage homeownership costs: $24,000/year
- Utility costs up 35% since 2022; insurance up 58% since 2018
- No standardized way to measure, compare, or communicate home performance at the point of transaction
- 86% of buyers want climate-resilient features; 80%+ consider climate risk in purchase decisions

**The result:** Buyers overpay for underperforming homes. Sellers can't prove the value of improvements they've made. Agents have no tool to guide the conversation.

## Slide 3: Value Proposition

**Pearl SCORE — the first national benchmark for home performance.**

- 1–1,000 rating across 5 pillars: Safety, Comfort, Operations, Resilience, Energy
- 92 million homes scored — every home in America has a baseline
- Not a pass/fail judgment — a framework for understanding and comparing
- Neutral: serves buyers, sellers, and agents equally
- "Performance on Rails" — puts the performance conversation into the transaction without blocking it

**For buyers:** A guide to discovery — framework, prioritization, and questions to ask
**For sellers:** A mirror — showing how the public record already sees their home, with control to correct it
**For agents:** Fewer surprises, cleaner negotiations, deals that close

## Slide 7: Competitive Analysis

**Pearl is the only national platform covering all 5 pillars for existing homes.**

| Competitor | Pillars Covered | Gap |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Kukun | Operations only | No Safety, Comfort, Resilience, Energy |
| Home energy auditors | Energy only | No Safety, Comfort, Operations, Resilience |
| Inspection platforms | Safety (partial) | No scoring, no standardization |
| Zillow/Redfin/Realtor.com | None (transaction focus) | Potential distribution partners, not competitors |

### Defensive Moat
- 10 years of industry relationship building — not replicable quickly
- Only national sponsor of DOE's Home Performance with ENERGY STAR
- Building science expertise in-house (methodology credibility)
- Verified database built through human verification — can't be assembled from public records alone
- Contractor certification network generates proprietary data streams
- B-Corp certified (accountability signal)

### Competitive Framing
- Competitors focus on 1-2 pillars → complementary, not threatening
- Real estate platforms are distribution channels, not competitors
- "Anyone can build this" rebuttal: 10 years + DOE + building science team + verified data = not replicable
