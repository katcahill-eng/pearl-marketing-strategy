## About Pearl

- Created the first national home performance rating system in the US
- Pearl SCORE rates all 92 million US single-family homes across 5 pillars

## Strategic Framework

### Two Audiences

**B2B: Real Estate Professionals (Authority Positioning)**
- Pearl is the standard for home performance data
- Helps agents answer questions buyers are now asking
- Transaction-facilitating, not transaction-blocking
- Target: Agents, brokers, MLSs, portals

**B2C: Home Buyers in Transaction (Demand Generation)**
- Pearl helps buyers understand what it's actually like to live in a home
- Creates pull that drives seller claiming behavior
- Target: Active home shoppers (not homeowners generally)

### Core Narrative

**The Historical Problem:**
For decades, real estate prioritized location, aesthetics, and price—while the daily lived experience remained invisible. Not because anyone was hiding it, but because the data was trapped in clerk's offices, too fragmented to access, too complicated to interpret.

**The "Why Now":**
- COVID changed how people experience their homes (air quality, comfort, remote work)
- Extreme weather events made resilience impossible to ignore (wildfires, storms, grid failures)
- Buyers are asking questions they never asked before

**Pearl's Role:**
Pearl built the first national infrastructure for home performance data. For the first time, the lived experience of a home is visible before you buy.

### Neutral Positioning (Critical)

**What Pearl IS:**
- The standard for home performance data
- Transaction-facilitating (helps deals close smoother)
- Neutral—we provide data, not advocacy

**What Pearl is NOT:**
- A tool to help sellers get more money
- A tool to help buyers negotiate down
- A judge of "good" vs. "bad" homes

**The First Street Lesson:**
First Street (climate risk data) was removed from Zillow despite accurate data because they positioned as authoritative/judging and were perceived as transaction-blocking. Pearl must be the opposite—an enabler, not a judge.

### Language Guidelines

**Use:**
- "Extreme weather events" / "environmental stress" / "weather events"
- "What the market sees" (for baseline scores)
- "Your data" (when discussing what Pearl shows)
- "Transaction-facilitating"
- "Operational now" / "live and searchable"

**Avoid:**
- "Climate change" (politically divisive)
- "Our data" (implies ownership vs. access)
- "Pearl helps sellers get more money"
- "Beta" in external contexts
- Leading with data limitations

### The Claiming Flywheel

The strategy depends on this behavior loop:
1. Buyer discovers Pearl SCORE on listing
2. Buyer sees home is "unclaimed" (incomplete data)
3. Buyer requests seller claim the home
4. Seller claims to avoid looking suspicious
5. Pearl gains verified data
6. Database improves → more buyer trust → more demand

**Marketing's job:** Create buyer awareness that claimed vs. unclaimed matters

## Marketing Approach: Moments, Not Months

### Core Principle
Concentrate spend around 4-6 major moments per year where Pearl dominates the conversation—rather than spreading budget thin across steady-state activities.

**Splash comes from concentration, not distribution.**

$24K spread across 12 months of LinkedIn ads = invisible
$24K concentrated in 2-3 major moments = memorable

### What Constitutes a "Moment"
- Major conference (Inman Connect, NAR)
- Partnership announcement
- Report launch (State of Home Performance)
- Tier-1 media hit
- Major market entry

### Surge Activities (During Moments)
- Geo-fencing (if event-based)
- LinkedIn ads at 5-10x normal spend
- Social content blitz
- PR push (coordinate with Atkinson)
- Email to full list
- Founder availability for interviews

### Between Moments
- Minimal spend on retargeting only ($500-1K/month)
- Organic content keeps presence alive
- PR continues pitching

Every activity must pass: "Does this create an asset, relationship, or system we can use again?"
